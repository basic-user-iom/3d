# How to Check if Cursor Cloud Sync Has Your Chats

## Quick Check in Cursor UI

### Method 1: Check Settings (Easiest)

1. **Open Cursor Settings**:
   - Press `Ctrl+,` (or `Cmd+,` on Mac)
   - OR: `Ctrl+Shift+P` → Type "Preferences: Open Settings"

2. **Search for Sync**:
   - In the settings search box, type: `sync`
   - Look for settings like:
     - "Settings Sync"
     - "Chat History Sync" 
     - "Cloud Sync"
     - "Enable Chat Sync"

3. **Check Account Status**:
   - Look at the bottom-left corner for your account icon
   - Click it to see if you're signed in
   - Check for sync status indicators

### Method 2: Check Cursor Website

1. **Go to Cursor Website**:
   - Open browser: https://cursor.com
   - Click "Sign In" (top-right)
   - Log in with your Cursor account

2. **Check Dashboard**:
   - After signing in, go to: https://cursor.com/dashboard
   - OR: https://cursor.com/account
   - Look for:
     - Chat History section
     - Sync settings
     - Account subscription status

### Method 3: Command Palette

1. Press `Ctrl+Shift+P` (or `Cmd+Shift+P` on Mac)
2. Type: `Cursor: Sign In` or `Account`
3. Check if you're signed in and see sync options

## What to Look For

✅ **If Cloud Sync is Enabled**:
- You should see "Chat History" in your account dashboard
- Previous chats might automatically restore
- There should be a sync indicator in Cursor

❌ **If Cloud Sync is NOT Available**:
- Chat history is stored locally only
- Once deleted locally, chats cannot be recovered
- This is common for free Cursor accounts

## Important Notes

⚠️ **Cursor may not sync chat history**:
- Depends on your Cursor subscription plan
- Free accounts typically don't have cloud sync
- Even paid accounts may not sync all chat history

💡 **If chats are not in cloud**:
- They were stored locally only
- Once deleted, they're gone
- Future chats will be saved normally (locally)

## Next Steps

1. **Check Cursor Settings** (Method 1 above)
2. **Check Cursor Website** (Method 2 above)  
3. **If no cloud sync**: Your chats are gone, but new ones will be saved
